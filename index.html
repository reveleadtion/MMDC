Michigan's Best Grassroots Drift Events — $10 Spectator Admission, Kids Free

The Mid Michigan Drift Club runs the most active drift event series in the state of Michigan, with seven events at Jackson Speedway from March through November 2026. Whether you're a longtime car enthusiast or just looking for something wild to do on a weekend near Jackson, MI — spectator admission is just $10 for adults, free for kids under 18, with free parking every event. Rotating food vendors are on site, or bring your own — it's a full day out without the full-price bill.

Our events range from beginner-friendly drift clinics to full open competition days, making MMDC the perfect entry point for anyone curious about grassroots motorsport in Michigan. Jackson Speedway sits at 3363 Page Ave in Jackson, MI — easy to reach from Lansing, Ann Arbor, and the greater mid-Michigan area.

About MMDC

✦ Our Story

Built for the Love of the Slide

The Mid Michigan Drift Club is Michigan's most active grassroots drift community — and we run it the right way. No velvet ropes, no gatekeeping. Just real cars, real drivers, and real people standing at a fence watching something they've never seen this close before.

We run a full seven-event season at Jackson Speedway, just off Page Ave in Jackson, MI — easy to get to from Lansing, Ann Arbor, or anywhere in between. Whether you're coming to watch for the first time or you've been chasing angle for years, there's a place for you here.

✦ First Time At A Drift Event?

Here's What to Expect

01

Show Up & Park

Free parking at Jackson Speedway. Adults are $10 at the gate — kids under 18 always get in free. No pre-registration needed for spectators, just show up.

02

Walk the Paddock

Unlike stadium motorsport, grassroots drifting has an open paddock. You can walk right up to the cars, talk to drivers, and see what makes a drift build tick.

03

Find Your Spot

The action at Jackson Speedway happens close. Find a spot at the fence near a corner and watch cars come in at full lock — loud, smoky, and completely controlled chaos.

04

Eat, Hang, Repeat

Rotating food vendors are on site at events — or pack your own. It's a full day out. Bring the crew, find a spot at the fence, and stay until the last run.
